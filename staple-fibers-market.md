<p><p>The staple fibers market comprises three primary types: cotton, cellulosic, and polyester. Cotton fibers are natural, offering softness and breathability, making them popular for apparel and home textiles. Cellulosic fibers, derived from plants, provide versatility and are known for their eco-friendliness, used in products like rayon and viscose. Polyester fibers, synthetic in nature, are valued for their durability, resistance to wrinkling, and easy care, commonly used in clothing and industrial applications. Each type caters to distinct consumer needs and preferences.</p></p>
