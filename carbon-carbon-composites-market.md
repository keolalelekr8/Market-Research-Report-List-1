<p><ul><li>Chemical Vapor Deposition</li><li>Liquid Impregnation Process</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Carbon-Carbon Composite Market primarily encompasses two main production methods: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Liquid Impregnation Process. CVD involves the deposition of carbon atoms from gaseous precursors onto a substrate, creating dense, high-performance composites ideal for high-temperature applications. In contrast, the Liquid Impregnation Process entails saturating a carbon fiber preform with a liquid resin, followed by curing, resulting in enhanced mechanical properties and tailored characteristics. Both methods serve diverse industries, including aerospace, automotive, and energy sectors.</p></p>
